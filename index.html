    Software and service recommendations

  • uBlock Origin

    This is the ad-blocker to pick on desktop browsers, given its resource usage and stance on privacy.

  • HTTPS Everywhere

    Automatically redirects to known HTTPS versions of sites.

  • Pi-Hole

    DNS level ad-blocker.

  • Adguard (iOS)

    Ad-blocker with EasyList integration.

  • DNSCrypt

    DNS is one of the weaker links in the Internet architecture today. DNSCrypt protects DNS traffic in the last mile (between your ISP and home network).

  • Messaging

    Signal, WhatsApp, or iMessage. Because they provide end-to-end encryption, by default.

    Note that, however, iCloud backup needs to be turned off for iMessage to be truly end-to-end-encrypted.

  • IINA (macOS)

    Video player designed for the Mac, with mpv as its backend.

  • Apollo for Reddit (iOS)

    An elegantly-designed iOS client to waste time on Reddit with.

  • NewsBlur

    Free RSS reader service with a clean design and without any BS (e.g. paywall, ads).

  • Unread (iOS)

    Clutter-free RSS reader with superb design.

  • Reeder (macOS)

    RSS reader with a solid design.

  • Overcast (iOS)

    Free podcast client that automatically trims silent gaps.

  • Dash (macOS)

    Nifty (offline) documentation lookup tool.

  • Aria2

    Robust command-line download manager with support for openning multiple connections per download task.

  • Air for Steam

    A Steam skin that makes the desktop client look much better.
